Masters of Optometry

DWC proudly offers a 2 year PG program in Bachelor of Optometry (B.Optom). In this course students get educated about the various types of visual disorders, conditions, diseases and abnormalities which affect the human eye. SKFGI offers one of the most engaging optometry program in West Bengal with an approach of focusing on practical problem based and case-based learning in order to improve clinical decision making ability in students and make them prepare for the ever challenging medical profession world.

Students are given opportunities to explore various possibilities and engage directly hands-on with patients via Eye Screening Camps and Observation Fellowship. We focus on the latest ophthalmic examinations techniques practiced in the eye hospitals. We upgrade the knowledge of our students through various seminars from practitioners via the online platform to boost the students with new determination during the pandemic times.For the final year of this 4-year long program, students are given the opportunity for internships. This in turn prepares the students with sufficient industry experience and provides them with the much needed exposure before venturing into becoming full-time Optometrists.

Eligibility

  • 10+2 with Physics & Mathematics along with any one of Chemistry / Biotechnology / Biology / Computer Science / Computer Application / Technical Vocational Subject and English as compulsory subjects with individual pass marks ( in both theory and practical wherever applicable) in all the three subject as stated above.
  • Must have obtained at least 45% marks ( 40% in case of candidates belonging to reserved category e.g. ST, SC, OBC-A, OBC-B, PwD) in the above three subjects taken together.
  • The eligibility for lateral entry is 10th + 3yr Diploma in relevant field or B.Sc. Degree from any university recognized by UGC with at least 45% (40% for SC/ST/OBC-A/OBC-B/PwD) marks.
